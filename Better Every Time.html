01

Thermal Shock

The Ice Cube Method. When anxiety spikes, your brain enters fight-or-flight mode. Holding a piece of ice shifts your brain's attention away from panic and forces it to process the intense physical sensation of cold.

Application: Hold an ice cube in your hand until it melts, or rub it gently along your wrists or the back of your neck. Focus entirely on the temperature and texture.

02

Hydro Reset

The Shower Mentor. Also known as the mammalian dive reflex, sudden exposure to cold water lowers your heart rate and redirects blood flow, effectively acting as a hard reset button for your nervous system.

Application: Step into a cold shower for 30 to 60 seconds, or splash freezing cold water directly onto your face. Breathe slowly and deeply through the shock.

03

Somatic Anchor

The Physical Method. Anxiety pulls you into the future or the past. Physical tension and release bring you violently back to the exact second you are standing in.

Application: Clench every muscle in your body—your fists, jaw, legs, and core—as tightly as you can for 5 seconds. Exhale sharply and release everything all at once. Repeat three times.
